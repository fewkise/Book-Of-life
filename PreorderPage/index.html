  • Story

  • The Birth of "Book of the Life": An Epic Journey into a New Realm.

  • In the bustling heart of Tokyo, amidst neon-lit streets and the hum of cutting-edge technology, a visionary team of developers and designers set out to craft a game that would redefine the shooter genre.

  • This ambitious project, known as "Book of the Life" (生命の書, Seimei no Sho), was born from a desire to blend intense action with a deep narrative rooted in Japanese culture and anime aesthetics.

  • The game explores themes of life, death, destiny, and the courage to face one's fears. As Hikaru unravels the secrets of the Book, he discovers that the true power lies not just in the tome but within himself and the bonds he forges.

  • The Creative Minds Behind the Vision

  • Leading the project was Hiroshi Takeda (武田宏史), a renowned game designer celebrated for his work on previous titles like "Eternal Blade" and "Cyber Samurai." Hiroshi's passion for storytelling and innovative gameplay fueled the development.

  • He envisioned a universe where players could immerse themselves in a richly animated world, filled with compelling characters and epic battles.

  • Inspiration and Cultural Fusion

  • The concept of "Book of the Life" drew inspiration from traditional Japanese folklore, modern manga, and anime. The game’s universe is set in a future Tokyo where spiritual energy and technological advancements coexist.

  • Players assume the role of Kaito (海翔), a young warrior who discovers an ancient, mystical book that contains the secrets of life and death.
